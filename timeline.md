<div class="milestone" markdown="1">
### 1933 — Roots in Bethlehem

<img src="{{ '/images/1940-or-41-jack-c-lail.webp' | relative_url }}" alt="Jack C. Lail in 1940 or 1941" class="feature-img-responsive">

Jack is born in a partial log cabin into a large farming family in Alexander County. It is the height of the Great Depression, and the same year the family farm is lost to foreclosure, setting the stage for a life built on hard work and perseverance.
</div>

<div class="milestone" markdown="1">
### 1939 — The School Years Begin

Starts first grade at Bethlehem School. Life is centered around Duke Power rental farm and later the "Bowman Farm." The daily rhythms of living on a farm in a rural area instill early lessons in the value of community and family.
</div>

<div class="milestone" markdown="1">
### 1952 – 1956 — Navy Service & Global Horizons

Enlists in the U.S. Navy and trains as an aviation electrician. His service inlcuded being assigned to a project involving a new plane being developed for the Navy. He was on a flight in a test plane that crashed on landing. Jack describes the harrowing experience of the plane hitting a structure at 80 miles per hour, which "sheared the wings off" and left the fuselage as the only part remaining. Remarkably, Jack and the others survived the impact. He later was deployed aboard aircraft carriers in the Mediterranean. This service broadens his perspective and provides the technical foundation for his future career.
</div>

<div class="milestone" markdown="1">
### 1954 — A Partnership for Life

<img src="{{ '/images/1954-12-jack-betty-wedding.webp' | relative_url }}" alt="Jack and Betty Lail on their wedding day" class="feature-img-responsive">

Returns to North Carolina and marries Betty Edwards of Hickory, North Carolina. This partnership becomes the cornerstone of his personal and professional life as he begins his transition into the industrial workforce. The family moves to Marietta, Georgia, where he goes to work for Lockheed Aircraft Corporation.
</div>

<div class="milestone" markdown="1">
### 1960 – 1970 — The General Electric Era

Joins General Electric in Asheboro. This decade is a period of intense professional growth, highlighted by a transformative business trip to Japan that reshaped Jack's views on manufacturing quality.
</div>

<div class="milestone" markdown="1">
### 1970 — Founding Mid-State Plastics

Jack embarks on his greatest entrepreneurial venture, co-founding Mid-State Plastics in Seagrove. He builds the company into a regional leader, prioritizing a culture of respect and integrity for every employee. The company expanded with the development of a plant in Round Rock, Texas, in 1990.
</div>

<div class="milestone" markdown="1">
### 1994 – Present — A Legacy of Leadership

After the sale of Mid-State Plastics in 1994 to Anchor Advanced Plastics, Jack shifts his focus to civic duty and travel with wife and friends. His leadership on boards, including Randolph Community College and Wingate University, ensure his impact continues to be felt across the state.
</div>
